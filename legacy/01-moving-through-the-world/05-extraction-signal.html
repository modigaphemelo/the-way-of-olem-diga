The Extraction Signal

What tells me it's time to leave immediately?

Why This Matters

The ASD nervous system does not always provide clear, real-time feedback about its own state. I can be fine one moment and completely overwhelmed the next, without passing through any recognizable intermediate stages. This is not a failure of self-awareness. It is the nature of a system that processes sensory and social input with a delay. The crash feels sudden, but the conditions for it accumulated over the preceding hours.

The extraction signal is a pre-identified marker that indicates I am approaching the point of no return. It is the early warning system that triggers the exit protocol before the crash occurs. Without this signal, I will consistently overstay my capacity and pay for it with hours or days of recovery. The signal is personal and must be discovered through observation, not adopted from someone else. My extraction signal will not be yours. Your extraction signal will not be mine. The work of this practice is to identify my specific, reliable indicators of imminent overwhelm.

The Principles

The Signal Is Physical, Not Emotional

Emotions are unreliable indicators for this configuration. The Fe inferior can produce irritability that is not related to sensory overload. The 5's detachment can mask overwhelm until it is too late. Physical signals are more reliable. A specific quality of tension in the jaw or shoulders. Sound becoming physically painful or startling. A feeling of pressure behind the eyes. Cognitive fog: words becoming difficult to find, thoughts feeling sticky or slow. These physical markers are the canaries in the mine. When they appear, the environment is becoming toxic regardless of what the emotions say.

The Signal Must Be Recognized Before the Crash

The purpose of the extraction signal is to trigger exit while I still have the executive function to execute a graceful departure. If I wait until the crash has fully arrived, I will not be able to extract effectively. I will be mute, frozen, or desperate. The signal must be calibrated to the early warning stage, not the full collapse stage. The early warning is subtle. It is often a slight increase in sensory sensitivity or a slight decrease in verbal fluency. Learning to notice these subtle shifts requires deliberate practice.

The Signal Triggers a Pre-Planned Exit

When the signal activates, I do not decide what to do. The decision has already been made. I follow the extraction protocol: deliver the neutral exit phrase, gather my belongings, and leave. I do not negotiate with myself about whether it is "really that bad." I do not weigh the social cost of leaving against the exhaustion cost of staying. The signal is the decision. The protocol is the execution. Removing the decision from the moment preserves the executive function for the exit itself.

The Protocol

  1. Review the last three social crashes. Recall the hours leading up to each crash. What did I notice, if anything, before the collapse? Did sound become irritating? Did I lose the ability to follow conversations? Did my body give any warning? Write down every potential early signal.
  2. Identify the most reliable physical marker. From the list above, select one signal that consistently appeared before the last crashes. This is the provisional extraction signal. It may be refined over time.
  3. Write the signal on the pre-departure checklist card. I add a line: "Extraction signal: [my specific marker]. When this occurs, exit immediately." The signal is now part of the mission parameters.
  4. Practice recognizing the signal in low-stakes environments. In the next few outings, I will simply observe whether the signal appears. I will not necessarily exit. The goal is to calibrate the signal against actual experience. Does it reliably precede overwhelm? Does it sometimes appear without leading to crash? The calibration improves the signal's accuracy.
  5. After the signal is calibrated, it becomes non-negotiable. When the signal activates during an outing, I leave. No internal debate. No external negotiation. The signal is the boundary. The boundary is the protection.

The Deeper Layer

The extraction signal is an act of trusting my own nervous system. The 5w4 configuration is prone to overriding bodily signals with cognitive analysis: "I should be able to handle this. It is not that bad. Leaving would be rude." The signal practice says: the body knows before the mind does. The body's warning is valid, even if the mind cannot articulate a logical reason for leaving. Trusting the body is difficult for the Ti-dominant mind, which wants evidence, justification, a coherent argument. The signal provides the evidence. The repeated correlation between the signal and the crash becomes the justification. The argument is made not with words but with data.

There is also a social dimension. Leaving an event early, or at a moment that seems sudden to others, can feel rude or conspicuous. The neutral exit phrase reduces the social friction. But the deeper issue is the belief that my needs are less important than others' comfort. The extraction signal challenges this belief directly. It says: my capacity is real, my limit is valid, and my departure is non-negotiable. This is not selfishness. It is the minimum necessary self-protection that makes any social engagement possible.

Reflection Questions

  • What physical sensations did I experience in the hour before my last social crash? What was my body telling me that I ignored?
  • What is one reliable early warning signal I have noticed in past overwhelms? Can I name it specifically?
  • When have I overridden my extraction signal and regretted it? What was the cost?
  • What neutral exit phrase can I prepare that feels comfortable and authentic to me?
