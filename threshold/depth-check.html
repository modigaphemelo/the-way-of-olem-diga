Depth Check

Before moving to Construction: have I faced my shadow?

I have inventoried my shadow.
I have mapped my pressure points.
I have traced the ripples of my actions.
But can I hold my own darkness without being consumed by it?

The Three Requirements

1

Honest Shadow Inventory

Have I named my hungers, my capacity for harm, and my ego attachments?

I know what I want so badly I'd compromise my values to get it. I know what someone could use to control me. I know where I need credit and where I can remain anonymous.

2

Pressure Readiness

Do I know what I will do when the pressure comes?

I have identified my breaking points. I have a story that holds. I know who my emergency contacts are—people paid to be on my side. I can wait while the wave passes.

3

Ripple Awareness

Do I map the consequences of my actions before I act?

I trace immediate, delayed, and emotional ripples. I identify witnesses I didn't see. I ask how this action will change how I see myself.

The Counterfeit

!

Counterfeit Depth

"I have ruminated endlessly and called it depth."

Introspection without action is not depth. Mapping the shadow without changing behavior is intellectual entertainment. True depth changes how I walk.

The test: "Am I hiding from action in introspection? Has this self-examination produced a single concrete change in how I operate?"

The Decision

Before I proceed to Construction, I ask myself honestly:

  • Have I named my hungers and my capacity for harm?
  • Do I know what I will do when the pressure comes?
  • Do I map the ripples of my actions before I act?
  • Can I hold my own darkness without confessing it, justifying it, or being consumed by it?

If I answered "no" to any of these: I remain in Depth. I cannot build something that matters while my shadow drives from the backseat.

If I answered "yes" to all: I am ready to build. Proceed to Construction.
